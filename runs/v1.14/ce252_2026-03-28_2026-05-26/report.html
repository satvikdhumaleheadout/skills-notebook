Current-year CVR (red) trends upward through the post window, while LY (grey) declined over the same period. Structural ΔCVR (after stripping seasonality) is +1.69pp vs apparent +0.72pp — the lift is real, not seasonal. Pre window starts 28 Mar.

CVR Improved — What's Driving It & What's Holding It Back

What drove the improvement?
CVR rose 3.46% → 4.19% (+0.72pp at CE level). Last year over the same late-March-to-late-May window CVR fell 1.07pp — the calendar transition into peak Paris tourism tends to dilute traffic mix. We rose 0.62pp despite that same seasonal pressure, so the underlying gain vs the seasonal baseline is +1.69pp — a structural improvement, not a calendar artefact. Within the dominant cohort MB · Paid · Google Ads ↗, S2C lifted +3.4pp and C2O lifted +5.6pp, anchored on the main Louvre product (TGID 3909) — 78% of select-page volume — where S2C rose +7.6pp and C2O rose +8.4pp ↗. C2A is the primary C2O sub-driver (+6.1pp on the fixed segment ↗); same/next-day availability improved measurably (median days_to_first_available 1 → 0 on top TGIDs ↗) — a direct lever for both S2C and C2A. Paid Google Ads quality lens corroborates independently: paid revenue +32%, ROI +17.8pp, CPC efficiency-positive (per perf-audit ↗).
What's holding it back?
LP2S declined −3.3pp at CE level (Shapley −47% ↗). Within the fixed segment, the headwind is broad across devices (iOS −5.8pp, Android −4.1pp, Desktop −5.7pp ↗) — not a device-specific UX regression. Two contributors: (1) the secondary MB URL paristickets.com/louvre-museum/ retreated from 11.3% → 4.7% of LP traffic, and that URL had higher native LP2S (61% vs 53% on the primary URL) — a routing effect that lowers the weighted average; (2) the primary microsite URL's own LP2S fell 53% → 48% on +1,663 user growth ↗. Per perf-audit ↗, paid quality IMPROVED (refutes a paid-dilution explanation), so the residual is consistent with an on-page browsing/engagement change rather than traffic quality.
When did improvement begin?
Gradual. No sharp inflection date ↗; the lift compounds across the post window with the strongest daily readings in mid-May. pre_period_healthy = TRUE (so the pre-window baseline is not depressed). The fact that LY declined over the same calendar window while we improved is what makes the structural gain meaningful, not just a recovery story.

6. Mobile / Conversion-Path Connection to Trigger

The CVR-RCA cascade fixed on Paid · Google Ads · MB. Two important observations:

  1. Aggregate paid CVR rose Post, so the mobile-paid CVR regression — if real — is a sub-segment effect masked by desktop/tablet gains, or by language-mix shift. The audit data here is desk+mobile blended; this audit cannot confirm or deny the MB-specific leak. The funnel would need to be split by device to localize it.
  2. No paid-acquisition cause is visible. Spend mix, campaign config, bid strategy, and traffic quality are all stable or improving. If the MB CVR leak is real, the root cause is downstream of acquisition (LP/checkout/payment on mobile), not in Google Ads campaign setup.

This is the key handoff to the parent RCA: paid traffic is not degraded; investigate the mobile conversion path itself.
