Root Cause

What broke?
Google Ads traffic to kennedyspacecenter-tickets.com fell 30% (92k→64k users) while Google Ads CVR held completely flat at 4.58%→4.59%. Nothing in the product or funnel broke — there are simply fewer high-CVR Paid visitors as a share of total traffic (71.8%→66.9%), pulling down the CE-wide weighted average.
Why did it break?
The traffic decline is largely a calendar artifact. Easter 2026's tourism demand — roughly 26k extra US domestic Google Ads users — was entirely concentrated in the final 6 days of the pre period (Mar 29–Apr 3), inflating the pre baseline. US domestic users (the core converting audience, 62% of traffic) show CVR essentially flat pre vs post (+0.08pp, 4.35%→4.43%), confirming no product or conversion problem for the audience that actually books. Post-Easter traffic settling at ~2,500–3,500/day vs pre-Easter normal of ~3,800–4,400/day accounts for the remaining ~15% structural volume shortfall.
When did it break?
Gradual, with a sharp Easter calendar distortion concentrated in the final 6 days of pre (Mar 29–Apr 3). The daily LP2S trend (see Section 3) shows the onset directly — LP2S collapsed from ~29–32% normal to 13.9% on Apr 1 as high-volume Easter browse traffic dominated. Post-Easter structural volume is ~15% below pre-Easter normal, consistent with a seasonal shoulder between spring break and summer.
