CVR Improved — What's Driving It & What's Holding It Back

What drove the improvement?
C2O improved +4.50pp (23.7% → 28.2%), accounting for 134% of ΔCVR. Both C2A (+4.45pp) and A2O (+2.58pp) improved. Desktop users completed bookings at dramatically higher rates (+6.78pp C2O on desktop), consistent with the Easter and spring season bringing high-intent planners to this bucket-list attraction. Prices for the main transport-inclusive tour (Exp 3370) also fell ~25% in the post period, reducing checkout sticker shock. Structurally, CVR is +0.82pp ahead of last year at the same calendar position (LY showed −0.45pp at this point).
What's holding it back?
S2C fell −1.13pp (23.7% → 22.6%), concentrated entirely in mobile: Android Mweb −3.55pp, iOS Mweb −1.18pp, while desktop S2C improved slightly (+0.75pp). On high-traffic Saturdays (e.g., Apr 25 = 2,219 LP users), the first available tour date was 3 days away — same-weekend mobile visitors found no transport-inclusive tour slots and abandoned the select page. S2C is already ~15pp below last year (22% vs LY 37% at same dates), a structural availability gap that has persisted throughout 2026. A new product (Exp 32872 — direct tickets, no transport) also entered the assortment at only 11.3% S2C, replacing a better-converting product.
When did the headwind emerge?
The S2C gap vs LY has been structural since at least February 2026. Within the post period, S2C worsened sharply from Apr 25 onward (17–20%) as Easter bookings filled near-term slots and the May Day bank holiday weekend drove high browsing volume from last-minute visitors who found no available tour dates.
