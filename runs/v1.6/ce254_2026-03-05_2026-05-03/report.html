Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 23.56% to 20.26% (−3.30pp, −14%), accounting for 188% of total CVR decline. The drop occurred across all date-specific single-day ticket experiences — 37399 (2-Park) and 10209 (1-Park) — with no impact on multi-day tickets, confirming the issue is specific to the date-picker step for near-term visits.
Why did it break?
Near-term Disneyland Paris dates sold out during the Spring holiday peak. The availability proxy (product_rankings_features) confirms min_days_available_30d dropped to 0 in the post period (vs a minimum of 27 in the pre period) — genuine date sellouts. Prices did not spike: the 1-Park ticket (10209) average price fell 8.2% ($70.65→$64.88), ruling out dynamic pricing. Users landing on the select page found no available dates within 0–29 days and abandoned. Near-term checkout buckets fell 20–27%; far-future bookings (30+d) held flat. The scarcity extended through the full Spring holiday period — not just Easter: non-Easter post days (20.92% S2C) were equally depressed as Easter week (21.21%).
When did it break?
Gradual onset from Apr 4 (post-period start), coinciding with the Easter holiday run-up. Sharpest single-day S2C: Apr 12 at 18.1% (Easter Saturday, 11,294 LP users — the highest-traffic day of the post period). Sustained low S2C through May 3 (17.7%). Structural decline: −0.27pp vs LY's +0.02pp in the same window — this is not a seasonal effect.

Daily S2C trend — pre vs post
S2C deteriorated from post-period day 1, with the sharpest drops on Easter Saturday (Apr 12: 18.1%) and late-April peak weekends. By May 3, S2C reached 17.7% — the lowest in the 60-day window.

Pre-period S2C was stable at 23–27% through early March, then declined through late March as Easter planning traffic (with near-term intent) began exhausting near-term inventory. Post-period S2C opened below pre-period average and continued eroding. Apr 12 (Easter Saturday, peak traffic day at 11,294 LP users) hit 18.1%. The pattern is demand-driven: on high-traffic peak days, more users arrive with near-term intent and encounter sold-out dates, depressing S2C further. The availability proxy confirms the sellout: min_days_available_30d = 0 in post vs 27 in pre. Crucially, the S2C depression persisted equally through non-Easter post days (20.92% avg vs Easter week's 21.21%) — confirming this is a sustained Spring holiday peak, not a single-event spike.
