Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 31.6% to 25.2% (−6.44pp), driving 70% of total CVR decline. The drop is broad across all three major experiences — UTV Raptor (−5.2pp on 3,960 pre select-page visitors), Movie Sites (−8.7pp on 1,983 pre select-page visitors), and Jungle (−10.0pp on 946 pre select-page visitors) — together accounting for 87% of checkout volume lost.
Why did it break?
Spring break wind-down: US domestic S2C fell −6.7pp on 15,711 pre select-page visitors; Canadian visitors fell −10.6pp on 1,053 pre select-page visitors. Both are the primary origin markets for Hawaii spring break travel. Non-spring-break markets (South Korea, Mexico) held flat or improved. Booking micro-funnel data confirms lower-intent visitor patterns across all three experiences — post-period visitors browse select pages but either don't engage with booking options or check availability and abandon without committing — consistent with a visitor cohort whose travel window has closed.
When did it break?
Gradual erosion from Apr 4 onwards — no single-day step-change. S2C drifted from ~31.6% (pre-period average) to ~25.2% (post-period average) over the full 30-day post window, with the steepest decline in mid-to-late April.

Lead-time Distribution — Booking Horizon Shift
Modest near-term reduction (−2.2pp in 0–2 day bookings), offset by a 3–7 day increase (+3.9pp). Pattern is consistent with a behavioural shift, not supply-driven displacement.

If supply scarcity in the near-term window were driving the lead-time shift, we would expect a sharp collapse in 0–2 day bookings alongside a spike in 7+ day bookings as users pushed out their trip dates. Instead, the shift is modest and the 3–7 day bucket absorbed the movement — consistent with the remaining (lower-intent) post-period visitors booking further in advance, not with unavailability forcing a rebook. Ruled out as an independent driver.
