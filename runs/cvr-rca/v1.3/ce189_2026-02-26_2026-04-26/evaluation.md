# CVR-RCA Evaluation
CE 189 · Vatican Museums | 2026-02-26–2026-03-27 vs 2026-03-28–2026-04-26 | 2026-04-27

## Overall verdict
The RCA correctly identified peak-season pricing increases (+3.5%–12.6%) and availability tightening as the primary S2C driver, grounded in actual product_rankings_features data. The C2O decomposition (both C2A and A2O declined, broad across devices) was handled accurately. The structural context was strong — correctly calling out that LY's larger drop was distorted by Easter 2025 and that structural_delta_cvr is positive. The main failure mode was stopping the S2C investigation one step short: the "broad across experiences" finding is accurate but does not distinguish between price-driven and availability-driven S2C abandonment — a price vs availability experiment (controlling for each) would have strengthened the causal claim.

## Theme scores

### 1. Narrative Coherence — 4/5
The hero section states a specific causal chain (peak-season pricing + availability → S2C → C2O) rather than just describing metric movements. The ruled-out sections (mix, structural deterioration) are explicit and cited with data. The report correctly omits language and page_type breakdowns because the investigation didn't find concentrated signals there. Minor deduction: the C2O story in the callout ("live inventory contention") is inferential — it's plausible but was not directly verified (no payment gateway log data). The callout should have used "consistent with" rather than asserting it as the mechanism.

### 2. Hypothesis Specificity & Quality — 3/5
L1 hypotheses were reasonably specific (Easter closure quantified, progressive sellout linked to daily trend, C2O sub-decomposition done). However, the S2C hypothesis never became falsifiable at the level the data would have supported: the investigation concluded "broad availability + price pressure" but didn't distinguish which experiences' S2C drops were primarily price-driven vs. availability-driven. EXP 9379 dropped −8.5pp with +6% price increase but only −1.8 days availability change — the price increase is likely the primary mechanism here, but this was never stated explicitly. The Vatican Gardens (EXP 16466) finding (days_to_first 10→21) was the one place where a specific mechanism was identified and stated clearly.

**Improvement:** For the two highest-volume experiences (6732 and 9379), form an explicit hypothesis: "Is the S2C drop driven more by price shock at the date picker, or by actual inability to find available dates?" Run a query checking S2C for users who viewed available vs. unavailable dates, or compare the lead-time distribution specifically for those two experiences.

### 3. Investigation Effort & Adaptivity — 3/5
The custom queries (experience-level S2C + availability, lead time, device × C2O, price) were the right set. The investigation stopped appropriately once the CE-wide picture was clear. However, after identifying the Easter closure dip and the Apr 23-26 sellout signal in the daily trend, there was no targeted query to confirm the sellout hypothesis — a query like "S2C by lead time bucket specifically for EXP 6732 pre vs post, excluding Apr 3-5" would have directly confirmed whether far-out date abandonment was the mechanism. Session recordings were attempted but failed (anonymous users) — this was correctly handled. The A2O drop (+2.4pp on Android) was flagged but not investigated with the order_attempted_events_v2 table, leaving an open item.

**Improvement:** Run S2C split by lead_time_days bucket for EXP 6732 specifically (not CE-level) — this would distinguish between "can't find any date" (short lead times doing well, long-lead S2C collapsing) vs "prices are too high" (uniform S2C drop across all lead times).

### 4. Branch Decision Quality — 4/5
The mix_dominance check was explicit and correctly ruled out (cited is_dominant = false + both segments showing conversion_effect dominant). The Shapley interpretation was accurate — S2C (78%) and C2O (62%) correctly identified as co-primary, LP2S offset correctly framed. The choice to drill experience-level S2C (rather than device or language first) was the right call given S2C was the primary driver and the CE has many distinct products. C2O sub-decomposition (C2A vs A2O) was correctly done before the device cut. Minor deduction: the device × C2O cut was run as a standard query rather than a hypothesis-driven one — no specific prediction was made about which device would show the signal before running it.

### 5. Evidence Strength — 4/5
All major claims in the report are backed by specific numbers with named sources. The Evidence inventory in findings.md is thorough with source attribution for every claim. The seasonal calibration (structural_delta_cvr, LY Easter distortion) was handled precisely. Confidence is correctly hedged where indirect: "consistent with live inventory contention" for A2O. Minor deduction: the Easter contribution (~0.3pp) was computed via back-of-envelope arithmetic on the daily trend rather than a direct query, and the method (approximating users_select from lp2s × users_lp) could be off by ±20%. It's directionally correct but cited as "Confirmed" rather than "Estimated."

### 6. Output Appropriateness — 4/5
Three charts (daily S2C, daily C2O, 90-day + LY) are all informative and each carries a distinct story — the S2C chart shows Easter + sellout tail, the C2O chart shows the post-Easter surge depression, the 90-day chart shows the structural context. The Shapley bar is correctly non-standard (LP2S shown as green offset rather than a standard blue bar) — a deliberate choice appropriate to the finding. The experience table combines S2C + availability + price in one view — good compression. Minor deduction: the lead time table appears in the report but its interpretive subtext is longer than the signal warrants — the finding (15+ days fell 4.7pp in share) is real but secondary; it could have been a single bullet in the ruled-out block rather than its own analysis block.

### 7. DRI & Actionability — 4/5
All three action cards name specific experiences or metrics: EXP 16466 availability collapse with exact days_to_first numbers, EXP 7998 price increase of $9.45 with explicit "check if deliberate", iOS Mweb C2A −4.2pp with "audit checkout form on iOS Safari post-Mar 28." The Vatican Gardens action (check API cut-off period settings) is testable and owned. The A2O/gateway action correctly points to order_attempted_events_v2. Minor deduction: the P2 action card (C2O) combines two distinct issues (iOS C2A = checkout friction; Android A2O = payment failure) under one DRI badge ("Payments / Engineering + Product") — these have different owners and could have been split into separate P2 cards for cleaner forwarding.

## Top improvements for next run
1. **Add a lead-time × experience query before concluding on the S2C mechanism.** "Broad availability pressure" is correct but underspecified. S2C split by lead_time bucket for EXP 6732 specifically would distinguish "no available dates" (far-out S2C collapsing) from "prices too high" (uniform S2C drop). This would make the root cause actionable: the former calls for supply capacity expansion, the latter calls for pricing review.

2. **Split C2O action into two cards by DRI.** iOS C2A (checkout abandonment before payment → Product/UX owner) and Android A2O (payment failure → Payments/Engineering owner) are different problems with different owners. A GM reading a combined card doesn't know who to forward it to without doing their own analysis.

3. **Query order_attempted_events_v2 for the A2O drop.** The A2O decline (−2.40pp) was flagged but not diagnosed. Breaking down failure_reason, payment_gateway, and fraud_evaluation_result_origin for the post period (especially Apr 6-9 where C2O was most depressed) would either confirm live inventory failure as the mechanism or reveal a gateway/fraud root cause that would change the DRI assignment entirely.
