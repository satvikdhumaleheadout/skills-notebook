CVR Improved — What's Driving It & What's Holding It Back

What drove the improvement?
LP2S rose +7.5pp (32.1% → 39.6%) as Keukenhof's tulip season peaked and Google Ads traffic doubled (+128% YoY). The paid traffic mix shift (72.7% → 81.2% paid share) brought higher-intent visitors, lifting overall CVR from 3.88% to 4.40% (+0.52pp / +13.4%). LP2S contributed +169.5% of ΔCVR — the improvement is real, but it is largely seasonal and mix-driven rather than structural (structural delta vs LY: only +0.09pp).
What's holding it back?
S2C fell −2pp (26.8% → 24.8%), a −62.5% headwind on ΔCVR. The primary cause: peak-season dates for Keukenhof Entry Tickets (TGID 10118) progressively sold out as April demand accumulated. Available days in the 30-day booking window fell from 31 to 15; 30d+ advance bookings collapsed 90% (751 → 77 checkouts). Without this S2C drag, CVR gain would have been ~20%+ rather than 13.4%.
When did the headwind emerge?
Gradual onset from Apr 2; availability measurably declining from Apr 11 onward. The Keukenhof season runs through mid-May — the supply constraint is worsening as remaining open dates are fewer and demand continues to arrive.
