Root Cause

What broke?
S2C (select-to-checkout rate) collapsed from 32.7% to 26.3% — a −6.4pp drop that accounts for 99% of the total CVR decline. LP2S held nearly flat and C2O improved slightly. The problem is exclusively in whether select-page visitors commit to checkout, not in traffic quality or order completion.
Why did it break?
A cohort shift after spring break. During Mar 30–Apr 14, Kualoa Ranch's microbrand site (kualoa-ranch.tickets-hawaii.com) served tourists physically in Hawaii booking for the current week — S2C 27–40%. After Apr 15, traffic shifted to future planners at home researching a Hawaii trip months away: they browse the date picker and click experience pages (LP2S holds at 40%+) but do not commit to checkout because their travel date is unconfirmed. Inventory is ample (avg 119 remaining slots in the 0–2 day window) — scarcity is not a factor. The change is in who is visiting, not in the product.
When did it break?
Gradual erosion in two steps: Step 1 around Apr 4–8 as spring break wound down (S2C drifted from ~35% to ~28%); Step 2 around Apr 14–15 as spring break ended fully (S2C dropped to 19–22% and continued deteriorating to 13–20% by Apr 25–28). A secondary event — the apparent delisting of the All-Inclusive Package (TGID 37863) from ~Apr 15 — further reduced checkouts in the final 14 days of the period.
