Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 27.9% to 24.5% (−3.44pp) across Vatican Museums, accounting for 63% of the total CVR decline. The sharpest drops occurred within Google Ads traffic on mobile devices, with Italian and European markets most affected. TGID 6732 (Vatican Skip-the-Line, the highest-volume experience) accounted for ~79% of the lost checkouts — its daily S2C hit 17.0% on April 25 (Italian Liberation Day) and 15.6–16.3% on April 30–May 1 (Italian Labour Day).
Why did it break?
Post-Easter demand composition shift. The pre-period (Apr 9–22) includes Easter week, when Vatican Museums attracts committed travelers with fixed plans and high booking intent. Post-Easter (Apr 23–May 6), the visitor mix shifts toward more casual European tourists and, on Italian national holidays, local day-trippers who browse dates but are less likely to commit to a booking. Italy traffic fell 22% in volume, while US visitors — who plan Vatican trips independently of the Easter calendar — showed only −0.9pp S2C decline. The same pattern occurred last year (structural delta ≈ 0).
When did it break?
Gradual onset from April 23. Two pronounced troughs on Italian national holidays: April 25 (Liberation Day) and May 1 (Labour Day). Partial recovery toward May 3–4 before the period closed.
