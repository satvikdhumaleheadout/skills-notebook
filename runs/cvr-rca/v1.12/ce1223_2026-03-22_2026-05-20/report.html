CVR Improved — What's Driving It & What's Holding It Back

What drove the improvement?
CVR rose +0.51pp (4.73% → 5.24%, +10.7%) with C2O carrying 86% of the lift ↗. Inside the dominant MB · Paid · Google Ads segment — 88% of paid MB volume — C2O climbed 48.9% → 52.7% (+3.80pp), with both C2A (55.1% → 58.4%) and A2O (88.7% → 90.2%) improving ↗. About half the gain is seasonal — LY shows the same +0.20pp spring-into-summer ramp — leaving +0.23pp structural ↗. The structural half aligns with deliberate Headout actions during the post window: MB assortment cap to 8 products on May 7, 5% last-minute discount on TGID 8869 from May 11, plus title and content updates on top TGIDs (per Enrico Samarughi · May 7–14 ↗).
What's holding it back?
HO (headout.com) is the headwind: traffic +38.8% but per-session CVR fell −1.23pp on weaker LP2S (−6.7pp) and S2C (−5.1pp) ↗. Net CE impact is small (≈ −0.04pp) and HO orders still grew +18% absolute — dilution from scaling lower-intent traffic, not a flow regression. Worth a Performance Marketing review of the new HO source quality before the next growth round.
Important context — different timeframe
Year-over-year is a different picture. CE 1223 GBV is −20% vs the same period in 2025 (FabriGPT WBR · May 18 ↗), and a May SP survey confirms the market collapse on the supply side: Tempio Travel −20/25%, Instazione −80%, Towns of Italy −45% — attributed to fewer US/MidEast/BR visitors and customers trading down from guided tours to entry tickets (per Enrico Samarughi · May 11 ↗). The CVR ratio gain in this report is real and well-earned, but it's happening inside a market that's shrunk YoY — read this as "we're converting better than last month," not "Pompeii demand is recovering."
When did improvement begin?
Gradual lift across the post window, no break date ↗. The strongest cluster of days (C2O 58–60%) lands May 13–16, immediately after the 8869 last-minute discount went live on May 11 — directional evidence that pricing lever is contributing to the structural half of the gain. Italy domestic volume rose +26.7% in the same window ↗, consistent with the spring Pompeii ramp shown on the LY overlay.
