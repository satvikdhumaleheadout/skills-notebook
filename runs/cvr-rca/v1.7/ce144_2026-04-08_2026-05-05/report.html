CVR Improved — What's Driving It & What's Holding It Back

What drove the improvement?
S2C improved from 22.9% to 29.9% (+7.0pp, 50% of ΔCVR) and C2O from 39.3% to 46.7% (+7.5pp, 33% of ΔCVR), driven by the April 15 launch of exp 36426 "Alcatraz Tickets with Ferry Ride & Audio Guide" at $47.95 — a $40 lower-priced alternative that displaced the $87.30 Self-Guided Tour App as the dominant Alcatraz listing. The lower price reduced friction at variant selection (S2C +7pp) and at checkout completion (C2A +9.9pp). International visitors benefited most: Canada +25pp, Australia +22pp, UK +8pp S2C — markets that had been deterred by the $87.30 price point. The structural CVR improvement is +3.78pp above last year's same-period performance, confirming this is a genuine product assortment gain.
What's holding it back?
Payment success (A2O) slipped from 88.0% to 85.7% (−2.24pp), slightly limiting the C2O gain. This likely reflects the shift toward more international visitors, who face marginally higher payment friction. All other funnel steps improved.
When did improvement begin?
Gradual step-up beginning April 22 (post-period start), one week after exp 36426 first appeared in the catalog on April 15. CVR remained elevated consistently across the full 14-day post window — this is a structural shift, not a one-day spike.
