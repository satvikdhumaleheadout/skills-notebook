Root Cause

What broke?
Select-to-checkout (S2C) fell from 30.8% to 24.0% (−6.83pp), accounting for 94% of the total CVR decline. The drop is concentrated in the UTV Raptor Tour and Movie Sites & Ranch Tour — the two highest-volume experiences on the CE — and the S2C decline on UTV Raptor is directly consistent with same-day and next-day ticket availability running at zero for 28 of 30 post-period days.
Why did it break?
The UTV Raptor Tour (the CE's highest-traffic experience) had no same/next-day tickets available throughout almost the entire post period — users selecting the experience encountered no bookable near-term dates and abandoned. Movie Sites & Ranch Tour shows a similar S2C decline (−7.46pp) despite healthy inventory, consistent with users who arrived intending to book UTV Raptor abandoning the funnel rather than rebooking onto alternative experiences. A secondary supply constraint is possible for Movie Sites on a small number of isolated dates.
When did it break?
The S2C decline opened at the post-period start (Apr 13) and persisted for the full 30 days. The daily chart shows S2C running consistently below pre-period levels from Apr 13 onward, with the sharpest values in the Apr 17–26 window. This is a sustained structural reduction, not a single-day event.
