Root Cause

What broke?
C2O fell 2.3pp (59% of the decline), driven by post-spring-break demand shift reducing checkout intent and by Magic Kingdom 1-Day Ticket (experience 36344) showing A2O collapsing from 64% to 48% — payment attempts failing while other WDW products held flat or improved. S2C also fell 2.7pp (27% of the decline) for the same seasonal reason: within the fixed segment (MB · Google Ads), S2C declined from 24.6% to 22.4% — the same demand-composition mechanism explains both steps.
Why did it break?
The pre window (Mar 13–Apr 12) captures US spring break at WDW — the peak of committed buyers. The 90-day CVR trend (above) shows elevated rates throughout the pre period, with a progressive step-down as spring break ends: LP2S fell 3.7% and S2C fell 9.0% within the fixed segment, confirming the funnel was less efficient in the post period at every step before payment. Separately, Magic Kingdom 1-Day Tickets show a sustained A2O degradation — inventory is unlimited (9,999 tickets per date, zero sold-out days), ruling out supply. The pattern is consistent with a WDW fulfillment or payment processing issue specific to Magic Kingdom single-day tickets from mid-April; the exact mechanism (API failure, fraud block, gateway degradation) is a data gap requiring order_attempted_events_v2 to resolve.
When did it break?
Gradual from Apr 13 — aligned with the end of spring break. No single break date; the funnel metrics deteriorate progressively as spring break ends, consistent with the 90-day trend. The Magic Kingdom A2O degradation is sustained across the full post period: weeks ending Apr 20 and May 04 were particularly low (38% and 40% A2O vs a pre-period average of ~65%), suggesting an intermittent or date-dependent fulfillment issue that began at the seasonal transition.
