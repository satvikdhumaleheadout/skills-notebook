# CVR-RCA Evaluation
CE 6495 · Kualoa Ranch | 2026-02-28–2026-03-29 vs 2026-03-30–2026-04-28 | 2026-04-29 (revised post inventory fix)

## Overall verdict
The investigation correctly identified the right-censoring artifact as the dominant explanation for the measured −1.00pp CVR drop, and definitively ruled out the initial inventory depletion hypothesis once the correct schema join was available. The final root cause is well-grounded: ~68% of the apparent decline is a measurement artifact; the genuine −0.32pp CVR decline is driven by a Google Ads LP traffic halving (−49%) and mobile S2C regression (iOS −3.7pp, Android −5.0pp, desktop flat). The pivot from a confident wrong answer (inventory) to a more nuanced right answer (right-censoring + paid traffic + mobile UX) was the standout moment in the investigation. The main weaknesses are that the right-censoring mechanism was not formed as a hypothesis early enough, and the channel breakdown leaves the cause of the Google Ads halving unresolved.

## Theme scores

### 1. Narrative Coherence — 4/5
The report leads with a clear and specific callout: measured −1.00pp is inflated; genuine decline is −0.32pp; two named mechanisms (Google Ads traffic halving, mobile S2C). The right-censoring analysis block is correctly placed before the daily trend chart so the reader understands the measurement context before seeing the data. Weakness: the lead-time table in Section 3 shows only avg_remaining and zero-inv columns — not the "dates with slots" columns specified in the report structure spec, which would allow the supply team to audit absolute slot counts. Minor omission.

### 2. Hypothesis Specificity & Quality — 4/5
The right-censoring hypothesis is properly falsifiable: "sessions in the final 14 days of the period have not had time to convert — restricting to Apr 1–14 sessions should reveal a smaller genuine decline." The inventory hypothesis was tested and definitively refuted before forming the right-censoring hypothesis. The mobile-vs-desktop S2C split produces a specific, falsifiable mechanism: "a mobile-specific UX issue at the select page or date-picker." The Google Ads hypothesis is partially confirmed (traffic halved) but the mechanism is left open (budget? bid strategy? Quality Score?). A sharper hypothesis would have been "Google Ads campaign was paused or budget was cut between Mar 29 and Mar 30" — that's testable with a 30-second look in Google Ads Manager.
**Improvement:** After confirming Google Ads LP traffic halved, the next step should have been to specify the mechanism hypothesis ("was this a budget cut?") and note the exact lookup action, not just hand it off to Paid Media generically.

### 3. Investigation Effort & Adaptivity — 4/5
The investigation correctly pivoted when the inventory hypothesis was refuted by the data — this is the most important adaptivity signal in the run. The right-censoring analysis was the right methodological next step after inventory was ruled out. The device breakdown correctly isolated mobile as the driver. The channel breakdown identified the Google Ads volume collapse. One gap: the channel S2C breakdown at the "Things To Do" level (−6.3pp S2C) raises a follow-on question — is the organic S2C decline a seasonality effect, a content change on the kualoa-ranch.tickets-hawaii.com microsite, or correlated with the same mobile UX issue? This was not pursued. A follow-on query filtering Things To Do sessions by device would clarify whether the organic S2C decline is the same mobile issue in a different traffic bucket.
**Improvement:** After identifying Things To Do organic S2C −6.3pp, cross-tab with device to test whether organic mobile sessions show the same drop as paid mobile sessions. If yes, the mobile UX hypothesis is strengthened; if no, there's a separate organic quality issue.

## Top improvements for next run

1. **Form the right-censoring hypothesis earlier.** Once the daily trend showed a smooth progressive decline through the end of the period — with the steepest drops in the final week — the right-censoring hypothesis should have been the first S2C investigation branch, before device and channel cuts. "Progressive end-of-period S2C collapse in a 30-day window" is a recognizable pattern; running the converged-sessions comparison should be the standard first step for any gradual late-period deterioration.

2. **Cross-tab the channel breakdown by device before declaring separate mechanisms.** Things To Do organic S2C declined −6.3pp and Google Ads S2C declined −3.0pp. Before writing these up as potentially different problems, a device × channel cross-tab would confirm whether both declines are concentrated in mobile (same underlying issue) or whether the organic channel has a distinct quality degradation. This would either consolidate the story (one mobile UX issue) or split it (two independent issues requiring different DRIs).

3. **Specify the investigation mechanism for Google Ads halving before handing off.** The action card correctly names the audit to run, but the investigation should have taken one more step: note whether the LP traffic drop was in impressions (bid/budget issue) or CTR (ad creative / Quality Score). Both are visible in Google Ads Manager without access to the account — asking a Paid Media team member to check "impressions vs CTR" rather than "everything" makes the audit 5 minutes instead of 30.
