Root Cause

What broke?
The measured CVR decline of −1.00pp (S2C −6.36pp) is substantially inflated by right-censoring — sessions started in the final 2 weeks of April haven't had time to complete checkout before the period closed. Genuinely-converged sessions (Mar 30–Apr 14) show only −2.79pp S2C decline, implying a real CVR impact of approximately −0.32pp. Inventory is confirmed healthy throughout — zero zero-availability dates and improving remaining capacity across all lead-time windows.
Why did it break?
Two independent causes drive the genuine −2.79pp S2C decline: (1) Google Ads LP traffic halved (17,345 → 8,855 users, −49%), contributing fewer high-intent paid search visitors and a modest −3.0pp S2C drop within that channel; (2) Mobile S2C declined on iOS (−3.7pp) and Android (−5.0pp) while desktop S2C held flat (+0.2pp), pointing to a mobile-specific UX or funnel issue at the select/date-picker stage.
When did it break?
The apparent late-April S2C collapse (38% → 13%) is the right-censoring effect amplifying over time — not a genuine worsening signal. The genuine mobile and paid-traffic declines were present from the start of the post period (Mar 30) and are steady, not escalating.

In a 30-day measurement window ending Apr 28, a session started on Apr 22 has only 6 days for checkout to register. Users researching a group trip, comparing options, or checking dates with family will simply not appear as conversions — they may still book days later, outside the measurement window. This right-censoring effect grows as the period-end approaches, producing the progressive daily collapse seen in the S2C trend chart. Restricting to sessions in the first half of the post period (Mar 30–Apr 14) gives each session ≥14 days to complete checkout, removing the artifact. The genuine structural S2C decline is −2.79pp, not −6.36pp.
