Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 29.1% to 26.1% (−3.05pp), broad across all top Vatican experiences, with tour products hit hardest (EXP 9379 guided tour −8.5pp). C2O also fell −3.34pp, with both checkout abandonment (C2A −2.66pp) and payment failures (A2O −2.40pp) increasing. LP2S improved +2.3pp, partially offsetting the impact.
Why did it break?
Peak spring tourist season (Mar 28–Apr 26) brought simultaneous price increases (+3.5% to +12.6% across top experiences) and tightening availability (main SKU EXP 6732 lost 2.2 available days; Vatican Gardens tour collapsed from 5.6 to 1.8 available days). Users reaching the date picker faced higher prices with fewer bookable dates — the combination drove elevated S2C abandonment. At checkout, peak-season prices created sticker shock (C2A drop), and live inventory contention as popular time slots were captured by competing buyers during payment processing (A2O drop).
When did it break?
Gradual onset from Mar 28 — no single-day step change, consistent with seasonal pricing and supply dynamics rather than a specific deploy. S2C accelerated downward in the final 4 days of the post period (Apr 23–26: 0.242 → 0.212) as late-April/early-May dates progressively sold through. Easter closure (Apr 3–5) contributed a minor additional dip (~0.3pp of the 3.05pp S2C drop).
