Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 31.6% to 25.1% (−6.5pp), concentrated in US domestic visitors (−6.7pp on 15,711 pre select-page users) and Canadian visitors (−10.6pp on 1,053 pre users) — together the primary spring break markets for this Hawaii destination. Non-spring-break international markets held flat or improved.
Why did it break?
Spring break season ended. The pre-period (Mar 5 – Apr 3) captured peak US and Canadian spring break travel — high-intent families and students planning Hawaii trips book with strong select-to-checkout conversion. The post-period (Apr 4 – May 3) began as North American schools returned, this intent cohort exited, and the remaining visitor mix shifted to non-spring-break international travelers who book at lower but stable rates. Supply checked and ruled out — all three major experiences had available tickets across every booking window throughout the post period. The booking window distribution barely shifted, consistent with a demand exit rather than supply-driven displacement.
When did it break?
Gradual decline beginning April 4 (start of post-period). S2C held near 28–34% in the first post week, declined to 19–27% in mid-April as spring break fully wound down, and troughed in the 14–22% range in late April. Pattern is consistent with North American schools returning on a rolling schedule across states and provinces — not a sudden product or supply event.
