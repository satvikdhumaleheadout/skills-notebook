Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 28.5% to 25.8% (−2.63pp) across all three main Vatican experiences — while traffic grew +12.9%. S2C is the primary driver at 64% of ΔCVR Shapley. A secondary C2O failure (40% Shapley) is addressed in the action cards below.
Why did it break?
Peak spring demand flooded Vatican Museums as Easter 2026 (Apr 5) and school holiday traffic drove a +13% LP surge, but the Vatican's timed-entry slot supply did not grow — Experience 6732 (the dominant product) lost 5.1 available booking dates per month (29.7→24.6), and near-term slots were fully depleted for Apr 6–24. Users who found their preferred dates sold out abandoned the select page rather than booking ahead. The secondary C2O failure splits across devices: Android A2O −4.6pp is consistent with live inventory race conditions (slots filling between select-page view and payment confirmation), while iOS C2A −3.3pp is consistent with the confirmed price increase on Experience 7998 ($79.88→$85.76, +7.4%) surfacing at variant selection in checkout.
When did it break?
Gradual and seasonal — S2C drifted down from late March through April as spring bookings progressively filled timed-entry slots. Easter Apr 4–5 created a 2-day transient spike in the CVR signal (CVR 2.6%–2.8% vs 4.5% baseline), but the underlying capacity pressure began before Easter and continued throughout the post period. The LY comparison is distorted: Easter 2025 (Apr 20) hit mid-LY-window, explaining the apparent positive structural delta (+0.008pp).

Calendar context — Easter 2025 vs 2026 and the structural delta
The positive structural_delta_cvr (+0.008pp) does not indicate genuine improvement — it reflects Easter 2025 landing in the LY post-window (Apr 20) with catastrophic traffic and near-zero CVR, artificially depressing the LY baseline.

Easter 2025 (Apr 20) fell mid-LY-post-window and drove massive unmonetisable traffic: Apr 20 = 42,200 LP users at CVR 0.80%; Apr 21 = 36,340 at 0.75%; Apr 25 = 34,608 at 0.57%. These dates massively inflate the LY denominator while contributing almost no conversions — pulling LY CVR far below the seasonal norm. Easter 2026 (Apr 4–5) hit only the first two days of the current post period (CVR 2.56%–2.84%), which is a much smaller distortion. The LY overlay chart above makes this pattern directly visible — the grey dashed line collapses to near-zero in the Apr 20–26 2025 window.

The structural delta formula (current_delta − ly_delta = −0.73pp − (−1.57pp) = +0.84pp) mathematically says "this year's decline is smaller than last year's decline in the same calendar window" — which is true, but only because LY was destroyed by Easter 2025. The correct read is: Vatican Museums CVR fell −0.63pp this post period versus pre period, and this drop is real and structural. The Easter transient on Apr 4–5 contributes a small additional noise signal; the ongoing capacity pressure from late April (Apr 25–May 3 S2C: 19–26%) is the lasting concern.
