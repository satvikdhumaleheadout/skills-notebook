Root Cause

What broke?
S2C fell from 31.8% to 25.2% (−6.65pp), accounting for 70% of the total CVR decline. The drop is CE-wide — all major experiences, both desktop and mobile, and every channel declined in parallel. No single experience or segment drives it; the entire select-page conversion base degraded together.
Why did it break?
Kualoa Ranch's visitor profile shifted away from high-intent spring break tourists (US families and students on planned Hawaii vacations who actively book activities) toward lower-intent off-peak visitors. Supply is abundant — zero sold-out dates across all experiences and all lead-time windows — and prices are unchanged. The decline is consistent with the natural end of Hawaii's spring break season, not a product or supply failure.
When did it break?
Gradual, two phases. Phase 1 (Apr 6–19): S2C eroded from ~32% to ~25% as spring break visitors tapered off — traffic held at ~4,200/week. Phase 2 (Apr 20+): S2C collapsed to 19–21% and traffic fell −71% as spring break ended for most US school districts and Hawaii tourism volumes dropped sharply.
