Root Cause

What broke?
Headline CVR is flat (−0.08pp) but this is a coincidence — LP2S dropped −2.68pp and was offset by an unrelated C2O lift (+2.5pp). The real cost is volume: LP traffic −11.5% and completed orders −12.6% (1,941 → 1,696). A single Google Ads campaign serving the kens-tours.antelope-canyon-tours.com subdomain — a high-CVR (~11%) MB microsite — lost 2,810 paid users (−46.6%) over the post window. That single slice accounts for ~294 of the ~245 net forgone orders (the rest cancelled by C2O gains elsewhere).
Why did it break?
A legal Cease & Desist from Ken's Tours (the underlying supplier) was received on May 6, 2026 (Sam Hyatt · May 6). In response, Performance Marketing executed Option B that same day-week: ad-group landing-page URLs were swapped from kens-tours.antelope-canyon-tours.com to the non-branded antelope-canyon-tours.com on May 7 (Deeksha Mundkur · May 7) and the subdomain itself was deleted on May 8 (Rajashree Das · May 8). The CVR collapse in the data is not a campaign incident — it is the measurable financial cost of an intentional, legally-mandated mitigation. Sam Hyatt's pre-action estimate of the revenue hit was $25-50K net rev/year; the May 14 MMP (Mallampalli Parinita · May 14) reports Kens went from ~30% of CE conversions at 7%+ CVR pre-LP-removal to 3% of conversions post-LP-removal — directionally consistent with this report's data.
When did it break?
Sudden break on May 7, 2026. Daily Google Ads → kens-tours dropped from 209 users on May 6 to 64 on May 7 — a single-day −69% drop — then 11 on May 8 and ~zero after. CE-wide MB daily LP traffic shows a corresponding step-down: pre-May-7 ~1,000–1,200/day; post-May-7 ~800–900/day.

Daily Google Ads → kens-tours timeline — the break date
Sharp break on May 7, 2026. Daily Google Ads → kens-tours volume fell from 209 users on May 6 to 64 on May 7 (−69% single-day), then 11 on May 8, then ≈ zero from May 9 onward.

The single-day drop pattern is consistent with a campaign pause, ad disapproval, or account-level policy action — not budget drift (which would show a gradual decline) and not bid-strategy auto-adjustment (which usually softens rather than zeros out). The orders line tracks the volume line one-for-one because CVR within the source held steady. From May 9 to the post-period end, this acquisition source contributes essentially zero — 12 lost days of ~220-users/day × 11% CVR ≈ 290 orders forgone in this window alone, with the same loss rate carrying forward until the campaign is restored or replaced.
